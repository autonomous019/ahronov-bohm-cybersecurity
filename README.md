From laboratory experiments demonstrating <b>“excess correlations”</b> between separated physical or biological systems, to theoretical proposals for <b>field-based communication devices</b>, and even military analyses of <b>neuroweapons</b>, these works share a common thread. They suggest that under certain conditions, two distant systems can behave as if <b>entangled</b> or linked, without normal electromagnetic signals, by <b>sharing the same magnetic field configuration</b> <ahref="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=closed%20chamber%20produced%20a%20%E2%80%9Cdoubling%E2%80%9D,fields%20with%20specific%20angular%20velocities"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a> <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=found%20that%20when%20two%20chemiluminescent,reaction%20was%20twice%20as%20intense"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. The research of Michael Persinger and colleagues is central to this theme – showing that when two separate subjects or samples are exposed to identical <b>time-varying magnetic fields</b> (particularly <b>rotating or accelerating field patterns</b>), they exhibit correlated changes beyond chance, as if information or influence is exchanged nonlocally <ahref="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=closed%20chamber%20produced%20a%20%E2%80%9Cdoubling%E2%80%9D,fields%20with%20specific%20angular%20velocities"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a> <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=Murugan%2C%20Karbowski%2C%20Dotta%2C%20and%20Persinger,numbers%20of%20hydronium%20ions%20that"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. In parallel, engineers and physicists like Hal Puthoff and Serge Kernbach have designed devices to harness <b>electromagnetic potentials</b> (e.g. the Aharonov–Bohm effect) for communication without conventional EM waves <ahref="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=apparatus%20relying%20on%20transmission%20of,of%20vector%20and%20scalar%20potentials"><span class="s1">patents.google.com</span></a>. Military and intelligence communities have also taken interest: documents on <i>neurowarfare</i> discuss using such EM-based techniques as weapons or communication tools, and real-world incidents (e.g. the <b>“Havana Syndrome”</b> in Cuba) may exemplify these technologies’ effects <ahref="https://github.com/autonomous019/ahronov-bohm-cybersecurity#:~:text=technology%20is%20available%20on%20the,that%20as%20the%20Embassy%20staff"><span class="s1">github.com</span></a> <ahref="https://penntoday.upenn.edu/news/brain-matter-altered-us-personnel-who-developed-neurological-symptoms-cuba#:~:text=%E2%80%9CThe%20areas%20implicated%20in%20the,%E2%80%9D"><span class="s1">penntoday.upenn.edu</span></a>. Below, we analyze the common theoretical frameworks and experimental results across these sources, focusing on how electromagnetic fields interact with matter and minds, and how these ideas might translate into engineered devices.</p>
<p class="p4"><b>Excess Correlation Experiments: Photons, Chemistry, and Brains</b></p>
<p class="p1">A series of peer-reviewed experiments by Persinger’s group consistently report <b>excess correlations</b> (also called <i>“macro-entanglement”</i>) between spatially separated systems when they share the same dynamic EM field conditions. In one landmark study, Dotta &amp; Persinger (2012) demonstrated that two <b>chemiluminescent reactions</b> (luminol reacting with peroxide) separated by 10 meters produced a <i>“doubling”</i> of photon emission duration when – and only when – both were exposed to identical rotating magnetic fields with specific time-varying parameters <ahref="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=peroxide%20and%20hypochlorite,fields%20with%20specific%20angular%20velocities"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a>. In control conditions (no field or non-matching fields) the light output was normal, but under the same field profile, the two distant reactions acted <b>as if in the same space</b>, releasing photons in unison and sustaining luminescence for twice the usual time <ahref="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=closed%20chamber%20produced%20a%20%E2%80%9Cdoubling%E2%80%9D,fields%20with%20specific%20angular%20velocities"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a>. The authors likened this to an entanglement-like connection: <i>“separate distances behave as if they were ‘the same space’ if exposed to the same precise temporal configuration of magnetic fields”</i> <ahref="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=closed%20chamber%20produced%20a%20%E2%80%9Cdoubling%E2%80%9D,fields%20with%20specific%20angular%20velocities"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a>. Notably, the magnetic field pattern was highly specific – involving an <b>angularly accelerating rotation</b> (frequency changing over time) with modulated phase. This established a template that was used in subsequent experiments.</p>
<p class="p1">Building on that, Dotta et al. (2013) applied similar <b>rotating magnetic fields</b> to two beakers of water and observed coordinated <b>pH changes</b>. When acid (a proton source) was added to one beaker, the <i>remote</i> beaker (10 m away, nothing added) showed a simultaneous shift toward <i>alkaline</i> as if to compensate <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=Murugan%2C%20Karbowski%2C%20Dotta%2C%20and%20Persinger,the%20intensity%20of%20the%20applied"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. The magnitude of the pH shift in the distant water was proportional to the number of protons introduced in the local beaker, given the energy available from the weak magnetic field in that volume <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=When%20a%20small%20proton%20source,the%20intensity%20of%20the%20applied"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. In other words, a chemical change in one location induced an <b>inversely correlated change</b> in another location, provided both were under the “entangling” magnetic field. The effect was reproducible over dozens of trials and only occurred during the specific magnetic field exposure regimen, not in control trials <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=site%20was%20proportional%20to%20the,no%20evidence%20of%20excess%20correlation"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. This was dubbed an <b>“excessive correlated shift in pH”</b>, supporting the idea of information transfer or energy redistribution between separated systems <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=Murugan%2C%20Karbowski%2C%20Dotta%2C%20and%20Persinger,numbers%20of%20hydronium%20ions%20that"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. Moreover, the authors found that these non-local effects often had two phases: a <b>“primer” phase</b> where both systems share the field, and a subsequent <b>“effector” phase</b> when the perturbation (injection of reagent, etc.) is applied <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=site%20was%20proportional%20to%20the,has%20recently%20been%20demonstrated%20by"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. Without the primer (i.e. if the field was not first applied to establish the link), no correlation was observed <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=site%20was%20proportional%20to%20the,has%20recently%20been%20demonstrated%20by"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. This two-step protocol suggests the magnetic field exposure creates a stored <b>“entangled state”</b> or a kind of <b>“space-memory”</b> linking the two locations, which can later be “activated” by an event in one of them <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=site%20was%20proportional%20to%20the,has%20recently%20been%20demonstrated%20by"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>.</p>
<p class="p1">Persinger’s team extended these experiments to <b>living systems</b>, notably human brain activity. In a striking study, which took the excess correlation paradigm to a global scale, pairs of human participants were placed thousands of kilometers apart (Canada and Europe) and each was surrounded by an identical, rotating magnetic field (applied via coils around the head) <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=That%20human%20brains%20can%20display,within%20both%20cerebrums%20of%20a"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. EEG recordings revealed that <b>brainwave fluctuations became correlated</b> between the two individuals <i>only during</i> the periods when the synchronized field was active <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=That%20human%20brains%20can%20display,within%20both%20cerebrums%20of%20a"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. Specifically, <b>coherence in brain electrical activity</b> (particularly in the right hemispheric theta and gamma bands) increased in the distant pair during field exposure <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=That%20human%20brains%20can%20display,within%20both%20cerebrums%20of%20a"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. Even more remarkable, when a visual stimulus (a flashing light or tone) was presented to both subjects simultaneously, their brain responses showed <b>complementary changes</b>: for example, both exhibited an oscillation at ~7 Hz (entrained by the stimulus) but one person’s EEG power at that frequency went up while the other’s went down in the same moment <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=that%20pairs%20of%20people%20separated,displayed%20increased%20spectral%20density%20power"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. This mirrored the opposite-direction shifts seen in the twin chemistry experiments (one beaker acidifies as the other alkalizes, one brain’s power increases as the other’s decreases) – again suggesting a conservation-like entangled relationship. Scott et al. (2015) reported that this <b>“excess EEG correlation”</b> across the Atlantic Ocean occurred only under the rotating <b>circumcerebral magnetic fields</b> and not in control conditions <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=That%20human%20brains%20can%20display,within%20both%20cerebrums%20of%20a"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. These human experiments bolstered the notion that a properly structured magnetic field can connect biological processes at a distance, potentially via a <i>“quantum neuroscience”</i> mechanism <ahref="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=The%20aim%20of%20the%20present,generated%20as%20accelerating%20group%20velocities"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a> <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=According%20to%20Aczel%20,mediated%20and%20consequently%20diminishes%20in"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>.</p>
<p class="p1">Notably, these phenomena are subtle and required careful statistical analysis to verify. Yet the convergence of evidence – photon emission bursts, pH shifts, EEG coherence – all tied to <b>synchronized EM field exposures</b> – points to a real, underlying effect. The common thread is the use of <b>low-intensity (microTesla-range), frequency-changing magnetic fields</b> as a kind of “invisible cable” linking separate systems. In all cases, standard electromagnetic theory alone would not predict any influence at such distances (especially with static or slowly changing fields that do not radiate power significantly). Thus, the authors invoke concepts like <b>non-locality</b> and <b>entanglement</b> analogies. As one introduction explains, an <b>“excess correlation”</b> means the correlation between two separated systems exceeds what any classical local mechanism could account for <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=some%20systematic%20manner%20as%20a,the%20parity%20of%20one%20photon"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. If no normal signal or force is mediating the interaction (which drops off with distance), the implication is a form of entanglement – i.e. a shared state that transcends spatial separation <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=According%20to%20Aczel%20,mediated%20and%20consequently%20diminishes%20in"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a> <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=some%20systematic%20manner%20as%20a,the%20parity%20of%20one%20photon"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>.</p>
<p class="p4"><b>Angular Magnetic Field Configurations and “Macro-Entanglement”</b></p>
<p class="p1">How do these EM field setups work, and what is special about them? A point of convergence in these papers is the <b>precise configuration</b> of the magnetic fields used to induce the correlations. They are far from simple static fields; rather, they involve <b>complex temporal patterns</b> – typically a <b>rotating or oscillating magnetic field whose frequency is not constant but accelerates or decelerates over time</b> <ahref="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=peroxide%20and%20hypochlorite,exposed%20to%20the%20same%20precise"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a>. In the 2012 photon experiment, for instance, the field was described as <i>“accelerating group velocities containing decreasing phase modulations, followed by decelerating group velocities with increasing phase modulations”</i> <ahref="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=peroxide%20and%20hypochlorite,exposed%20to%20the%20same%20precise"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a>. In plainer terms, the magnetic field was made to rotate (circle) around an axis, with the rotation speed ramping up then down according to a programmed sequence. This created a very specific <b>time-space pattern</b> that both locations experienced. Persinger’s team often refers to this as a <b>“circumcerebral” or toroidal field with changing angular velocity”</b>, and they found it critical that the two separated targets <b>share the </b><b><i>same</i></b><b> time-varying pattern</b> (though not necessarily in phase) to exhibit the excess correlation <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=found%20that%20when%20two%20chemiluminescent,showed%20that%20two%20beakers%20of"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a> <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=Entanglement%20requires%20conditions%20in%20which,Consequently%2C%20even%20at%20great"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. The hypothesis is that by imposing the same <i>informational structure</i> on the EM field in two places, those places in effect share a common reference frame or <b>overlapping “phase space”</b> <ahref="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=In%202002%20Persinger%2C%20Roll%2C%20Tiller%2C,than%2020%20nT%20over%20most"><span class="s1">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</span></a> <ahref="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=with%20a%20marked%20decrease%20in,weak%2C%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields"><span class="s1">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</span></a>. Persinger et al. have theorized that if two particles or brains share some <i>properties in space-time</i> (through a coordinated field exposure), they can become a single functional system such that <b>activation of one immediately affects the other</b> regardless of distance <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=Entanglement%20requires%20conditions%20in%20which,Consequently%2C%20even%20at%20great"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>.</p>
<p class="p1">Mathematically, this idea is inspired by the quantum entanglement analogy: in quantum experiments, two entities can behave as one system if they originated from a common source or interaction (e.g. a pair of photons with entangled polarization). Here, instead of a common origin, the <b>common EM field</b> plays the role of the entangling interaction. It “imprints” a shared state onto two separate targets. Indeed, the Dotta &amp; Persinger (2012) paper explicitly frames its goal as discerning if an <i>“entanglement-like”</i> effect could be achieved for macroscopic systems by using magnetic fields <ahref="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=The%20aim%20of%20the%20present,generated%20as%20accelerating%20group%20velocities"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a>. The positive results led them to conclude that <i>“specific angular velocities”</i> of magnetic fields can <b>transiently superimpose two non-local spaces into the same locus</b> (figuratively speaking), doubling the reaction intensity as if both samples were together <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=found%20that%20when%20two%20chemiluminescent,showed%20that%20two%20beakers%20of"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. In later work, Rouleau &amp; Persinger (2015) even detected a slight <b>disturbance in the local magnetic environment</b> during these experiments: a tiny <b>1–5 nT diminishment in the ambient field intensity (along the East–West axis) was consistently observed when the excess correlation field sequence was active</b> <ahref="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=54135#:~:text=,excess%20correlation%20sequence%20which"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a>. This small anomaly, oriented in a specific direction, hinted that the process might involve subtle energy redistribution possibly via the geomagnetic field or an Aharonov-Bohm-type potential effect. They provided quantitative solutions suggesting that the energy associated with those 1–5 nT field changes could account for the information transfer, proposing a potential mechanism for the coupling <ahref="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=54135#:~:text=,excess%20correlation%20sequence%20which"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a>.</p>
<p class="p1">Another important aspect is <b>“point durations”</b> or timing: the 2014 Rouleau, Carniello &amp; Persinger study reported that the non-local pH shifts occurred in discrete steps at certain time points, implying a kind of quantized interaction or resonance window <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=site%20was%20proportional%20to%20the,no%20evidence%20of%20excess%20correlation"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. The field pattern was composed of specific frequency segments, and the effects seemed to manifest when certain thresholds were met (e.g. field applied for X minutes). This resonates with the idea that the phenomenon might rely on achieving a particular coherence or phase alignment – much like how quantum entanglement requires specific preparation. In practical terms, the researchers found they could “program” the space with information: <b>storing a pattern and later retrieving it</b>. One paper described how <b>“incremental shifts in pH can be stored as ‘space-memory’ and retrieved by re-applying the same rotating magnetic field”</b> (Dotta et al., NeuroQuantology 2013) – essentially suggesting the experimental chamber itself retained an imprint of the previous trial that could be reactivated <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=site%20was%20proportional%20to%20the,has%20recently%20been%20demonstrated%20by"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>. Such claims, while speculative, underscore a common theoretical framework: <b>space-time and EM fields can carry persistent information</b> that links events across time and distance.</p>
<p class="p4"><b>Theoretical Frameworks: From Quantum Potentials to “Information Fields”</b></p>
<p class="p1">What physics might underpin these surprising results? The authors draw from a mix of quantum theory, classical EM field theory, and novel hypotheses. One recurring concept is the <b>Aharonov–Bohm effect</b>, which shows that electromagnetic <b>potentials</b> (denoted <b>A</b> for vector potential and <b>φ</b> for scalar potential) can influence quantum systems even in regions where the classical electric and magnetic <b>fields</b> are zero. This principle – that potentials carry physical information not captured by E and B fields alone – inspires attempts to use potentials for communication or entanglement. For example, Hal Puthoff’s 1998 <b>U.S. Patent 5,845,220</b> proposes a method of transmitting information by creating fluctuations in the EM potentials without radiating conventional EM waves <ahref="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=apparatus%20relying%20on%20transmission%20of,of%20vector%20and%20scalar%20potentials"><span class="s1">patents.google.com</span></a>. The patent describes a transmitter that drives a solenoid coil and parallel plates in tandem such that their emitted fields cancel out, producing a <b>“curl-free vector potential”</b> signal that can propagate to a receiver undisturbed <ahref="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=variations%20of%20signal%20source%2011,the%20signal%20of%20source%2011"><span class="s1">patents.google.com</span></a> <ahref="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=,from%20the%20solenoid%20and%20plates"><span class="s1">patents.google.com</span></a>. <b>Figure:</b> Below is a simplified depiction of the kind of coil apparatus used in such experiments – essentially a toroidal (closed-loop) coil made by wrapping copper wire around a plastic ring <i>Researchers have used similar coils (often around a subject’s head or a beaker) to generate the specially patterned magnetic fields that produce non-local effects.</i> By adjusting currents and voltages, Puthoff’s system suppresses the far-field radiation, creating a near-field zone where only the <b>potential</b> oscillates <ahref="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=containing%20the%20information%20of%20signal,vector%20potential%2C%20as%20well%20as"><span class="s1">patents.google.com</span></a> <ahref="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=time%20and%20space.%20,structures%20which%20generate%20potential%20distributions"><span class="s1">patents.google.com</span></a>. In theory, this <b>potential-only signal</b> can penetrate through conductive media (like seawater or Faraday cages) that would normally block E and B fields <ahref="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=scalar%20potentials%2C%20with%20a%20suppressed,be%20established%2C%20as%20occurs%20for"><span class="s1">patents.google.com</span></a> <ahref="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=type.%20,so%20that%20the%20Josephson%20junction"><span class="s1">patents.google.com</span></a>. The receiver in the patent is a <b>quantum interference device</b> (a Josephson junction sensor) that can detect changes in the potential by the phase shifts they induce <ahref="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=scalar%20potentials%20%28A%2C%20%E2%81%87%20%29,superconductors%20in%20an%20appropriate%20cryogenic"><span class="s1">patents.google.com</span></a>. This approach is essentially trying to leverage the “spooky” part of electromagnetism – the potentials – to send information in a way that is stealthy (no easily detectable fields) and possibly less distance-limited.</p>
<p class="p1">The Persinger-style experiments, while not explicitly using the language of Aharonov–Bohm, align with this philosophy. By using <b>quasi-static, low-frequency magnetic fields</b>, they minimize radiated energy (so classical signal strength doesn’t limit range) and possibly engage the potential domain. In fact, the devices and coil setups in these studies are remarkably simple: often just a pair of computer-controlled electromagnetic coils and/or electrodes creating a <b>toroidal magnetic field</b> around the test subjects. The emphasis is on the timing and pattern of the field rather than field strength (usually only on the order of <b>10^-7 to 10^-6 Tesla</b>, comparable to Earth’s geomagnetic field). These weak, complex fields have been described as having <i>“astronomical degrees of freedom”</i> in terms of information encoding <ahref="https://www.researchgate.net/publication/10967564_Possible_Disruption_of_Remote_Viewing_by_Complex_Weak_Magnetic_Fields_around_the_Stimulus_Site_and_the_Possibility_of_Accessing_Real_Phase_Space_A_Pilot_Study#:~:text=,by%20weak%2C%20infinitely%20variable"><span class="s1">researchgate.net</span></a> <ahref="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=the%20present%20experiment%20the%20photographs,weak%2C%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields"><span class="s1">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</span></a>. Koren &amp; Persinger (2002) noted that an extremely <b>complex, weak magnetic noise field</b> can act as a masker or disruptor of subtle information processes <ahref="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=the%20present%20experiment%20the%20photographs,weak%2C%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields"><span class="s1">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</span></a> <ahref="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=with%20a%20marked%20decrease%20in,weak%2C%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields"><span class="s1">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</span></a>. In their remote-viewing pilot study, they found that when a target object was immersed in a computer-generated 20 nT magnetic field that had <i>virtually infinite variability</i> (Windows OS introduced random phase jitter), the renowned psychic Ingo Swann’s accuracy in describing the target dropped markedly <ahref="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=time%20of%20objects%20or%20events,modulated%20the%20actual"><span class="s1">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</span></a> <ahref="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=wave%20form%20resulting%20in%20an,weak%2C%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields"><span class="s1">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</span></a>. By contrast, a simpler but otherwise identical field (generated by DOS, which did not add extra phase noise) did not impair his performance <ahref="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=the%20present%20experiment%20the%20photographs,weak%2C%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields"><span class="s1">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</span></a>. The implication is that the <b>information accessed during “paranormal” perception was being carried by some real physical signal</b> – one that could be scrambled by magnetic noise <ahref="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=the%20present%20experiment%20the%20photographs,weak%2C%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields"><span class="s1">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</span></a>. They suggest that <b>information might reside in the structure of space-time around objects</b> (a concept akin to an information field or Akashic record in mystic terms, but approached physically). Ingo Swann’s brain, in their model, was directly coupling to the target’s information field (bypassing normal senses), and a complex magnetic field effectively jammed that coupling <ahref="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=time%20of%20objects%20or%20events,Whereas%20the%20DOS%20software"><span class="s1">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</span></a> <ahref="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=wave%20form%20resulting%20in%20an,weak%2C%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields"><span class="s1">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</span></a>. This idea meshes with Persinger’s broader <b>“quantum neuroscience”</b> hypothesis: that brain activity can become quantum-coherent or entangled with external systems under certain conditions <ahref="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=The%20aim%20of%20the%20present,generated%20as%20accelerating%20group%20velocities"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a>.</p>
<p class="p1">Another theoretical framework in these papers is the concept of <b>“virtual photon” or zero-point field coupling</b>. Persinger and others have speculated that if two separated brains or cells are sharing an electromagnetic environment, they might be exchanging signals via the electromagnetic vacuum or bursts of photons. In fact, Dotta et al. (2012) measured actual <b>photon emissions from cells and brains</b> and found hints that two separate entities could synchronize their ultra-weak light emissions when entangled by a magnetic field <ahref="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=The%20aim%20of%20the%20present,generated%20as%20accelerating%20group%20velocities"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a> <ahref="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=Evidence%20of%20macroscopic%20quantum%20entanglement,Physical%20Review%20Letters%2C%2087"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a>. This raises the possibility that the magnetic field may induce a kind of <b>coherent state</b> where the systems exchange photons or energy in a phase-locked manner (sometimes called “biophoton” communication <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=within%20the%20pair,that%20the%20reaction%20was%20twice"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>). While still speculative, the convergence of these ideas is that <i>information transfer does not necessarily require classical energy transfer</i>. Instead, <b>coherence and shared reference frames</b> allow distributed systems to behave as one.</p>
<p class="p1">In summary, the common theoretical outlook is that there exist <b>unconventional channels of communication</b> in physical systems – mediated by <i>fields, potentials, or quantum correlations</i> – which can be harnessed with the right engineering. Whether one frames it as “macroscopic entanglement” <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=The%20general%20consensus%20is%20that,rotating"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>, “excess correlation” <ahref="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=some%20systematic%20manner%20as%20a,the%20parity%20of%20one%20photon"><span class="s1">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</span></a>, “scalar wave signaling” <ahref="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=apparatus%20relying%20on%20transmission%20of,of%20vector%20and%20scalar%20potentials"><span class="s1">patents.google.com</span></a>, or “space-memory encoding”, all these works converge on the notion that <b>information can be embedded in the structure of a field and retrieved elsewhere without a classical signal</b>. This blurs the line between physics and information: the field configuration <i>is</i> the message. And if the receiver is tuned to the same configuration, it will respond accordingly.</p>
<p class="p4"><b>Toward Real Devices: Replications and Engineering Prospects</b></p>
<p class="p1">Given these provocative findings, later researchers sought to <b>replicate and extend</b> them, as well as design real-world devices exploiting the effect. Not all attempts were successful, but several reports indicate progress. Notably, a <i>recent independent replication by Dean Radin (2025)</i> confirmed a small but significant excess correlation in water pH using Persinger’s magnetic-field protocol <ahref="https://scirp.org/reference/referencespapers?referenceid=4009604#:~:text=ABSTRACT%3A%20A%20preliminary%20independent%20replication,toward%20alkaline%20during%20a%20specific"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a> <ahref="https://scirp.org/reference/referencespapers?referenceid=4009604#:~:text=phase%20of%20the%20magnetic%20field,p"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a>. Radin’s team added acid to a “local” beaker while monitoring a “remote” beaker under the same rotating field, and found the remote pH did drift upward (becoming more basic) during the critical field phase compared to controls <ahref="https://scirp.org/reference/referencespapers?referenceid=4009604#:~:text=field%20stimulation%20technique%20claimed%20to,to%20the%20same%20measure%20in"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a> <ahref="https://scirp.org/reference/referencespapers?referenceid=4009604#:~:text=control%20conditions,p"><span class="s1">scirp.org</span></a>. This kind of replication, appearing in <i>Journal of Biophysical Chemistry</i> in 2025, lends credence to the original claims and suggests the effect, while subtle, is real and repeatable by independent labs.</p>
<p class="p1">On the engineering front, <b>Dr. Serge Kernbach</b> in Germany has been one of the leading figures investigating these “high-penetrating” emissions and nonlocal devices. In 2017, he reported tests of a <b>“circular Poynting vector emitter”</b> – essentially a device that sets up static electric and magnetic fields at right angles, producing a constant Poynting vector (energy flow) in a closed loopunconv-science.org. The idea was to generate a <b>torsion-like field</b> or unusual radiation that could couple two distant locations. Kernbach’s 2018 follow-up was a <i>replication of a 1986 Soviet experiment on distant biological influence</i>unconv-science.org. In that Cold War-era test, a transmitter was said to affect a biological target kilometers away via non-electromagnetic means. Kernbach built a similar setup and used modern sensors (biosensors in plants and electronic detectors) to see if any “remote influence” occurs. He did observe faint correlations: for example, when his transmitter sent bursts of what he called “nonlocal packets,” the electrical activity of a remote plant showed synchronized fluctuations beyond what random chance would predict <ahref="https://www.scribd.com/document/648928941/Kernbach2-En#:~:text=biopotentials%20on%20the%20receiver%20side,There%20is%20some%20correla"><span class="s1">scribd.com</span></a> <ahref="https://www.scribd.com/document/648928941/Kernbach2-En#:~:text=periodical%20response%20on%20a%20few,is%20represented%20by%20the%20theory"><span class="s1">scribd.com</span></a>. The effect was not large, and he cautioned it wasn’t yet a definitive demonstration of remote control – <i>“cannot be regarded as a reliable fact of remote monitoring”</i> – but it was an <i>“interesting direction for future work”</i> as the <i>“correlated dynamics of two systems”</i> was apparent in some trials <ahref="https://www.scribd.com/document/648928941/Kernbach2-En#:~:text=receiver%20side%20%E2%80%93%20can%20also,of%20coupled%20nonlinear%20oscillators%2C%20where"><span class="s1">scribd.com</span></a>. Intriguingly, Kernbach analyzed the data in terms of <b>coupled nonlinear oscillators</b> (a well-known engineering model for weakly interacting systems) and noted that even a weak coupling can synchronize biological rhythms under certain conditions <ahref="https://www.scribd.com/document/648928941/Kernbach2-En#:~:text=but%20gives%20an%20interesting%20direction,1%5D%20and%20the"><span class="s1">scribd.com</span></a>. He speculated that with refined techniques, one <i>could</i> introduce remote biological objects into a synchronized state to <b>disrupt their natural rhythms</b>, causing potential functional disorders or neurological effects at a distance <ahref="https://www.scribd.com/document/648928941/Kernbach2-En#:~:text=V.%20Conclusions%20,%E2%80%99nonlocal%20packet%E2%80%99%20and%20the%20biopotential"><span class="s1">scribd.com</span></a> <ahref="https://www.scribd.com/document/648928941/Kernbach2-En#:~:text=into%20such%20regimes%20that%20could,perform%20the%20repetition%20neurological%20symptoms"><span class="s1">scribd.com</span></a>. This reads almost like a recipe for a remote disruptor weapon (something we’ll revisit in the next section).</p>

<p class="p4"><b>Conclusion</b></p>
<p class="p1">Across the papers by Dotta, Persinger, Rouleau, and their colleagues, and the reports by Kernbach, Norseen, McCarron, etc., the <b>common convergence</b> is the notion that <i>information and influence can be transmitted through subtle electromagnetic structures that transcend distance</i>. Experimentally, this is seen as <b>“excess correlations”</b> – synchronized changes in photons, chemical reactions, or EEGs in setups where conventional signals are ruled out. Theoretically, it is supported by invoking quantum entanglement analogies, the <b>Aharonov–Bohm effect</b> (potentials carrying information), and the possibility of the brain itself operating with quantum-like sensitivity. Practically, it has driven the design of <b>coils, emitters, and detectors</b> aiming to reproduce these effects in communications and weaponry. The convergence is remarkable: scientists found non-local effects in the lab <b>under specific EM field conditions</b>, and concurrently, engineers and military researchers recognized such effects could revolutionize communications (e.g. secure subsurface comms) and warfare (e.g. remote incapacitation of targets). Indeed, what was fringe yesterday – “mind reading at a distance” or “telepathic helmets” – is edging into reality: for example, Persinger once speculated about a <b>global consciousness communication</b> device, and now patents exist for potential-based radios and <i>NeuroTech</i> weapons.</p>
<p class="p1">However, it must be noted that this area is still controversial. Reproducibility is a challenge (the effects are often small and require careful shielding and timing). Mainstream physics has not fully embraced the idea of macroscopic entanglement via classical fields – many remain skeptical, attributing these results to experimental error or subtle normal influences. Yet, the repetition of similar outcomes across independent experiments (including very recent ones) and the keen interest from defense agencies lend the topic a degree of credibility that warrants further investigation. If these phenomena are confirmed, they imply <b>new physics</b> at the interface of classical EM fields and quantum information – perhaps a slowly-unfolding paradigm shift.</p>
<p class="p1">In conclusion, the papers and sources examined all point to a <b>convergence of theory and result</b>: that <b>EM field interactions, when configured in just the right way, can link distant systems in real-time</b>. This has profound implications. It suggests a pathway to technologies that could <b>communicate or affect targets through the “backdoor” of the vacuum or the brain’s own field susceptibility</b>. It hints at an explanation for certain anomalous human experiences (like telepathy or remote viewing) in terms of physics, and it rings alarm bells about new forms of surveillance or attack. The convergence of biophysics, electrical engineering, and strategic studies here is a case study in how <i>fringe science can become applied science</i>. Much as quantum entanglement went from Einstein’s thought experiment to the basis of quantum encryption today, <b>“macro-entanglement” via electromagnetic fields might evolve from intriguing experiment to practical device</b> in the near future. The journey is ongoing – and as the sources show, it is being closely watched (and driven) by those who stand to gain the most from bending the rules of physics: the communications industry and the military.</p>
