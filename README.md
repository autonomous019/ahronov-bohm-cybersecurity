This mirrored the opposite-direction shifts seen in the twin chemistry experiments (one beaker acidifies as the other alkalizes, one brain‚Äôs power increases as the other‚Äôs decreases) ‚Äì again suggesting a conservation-like entangled relationship. Scott et al. (2015) reported that this </span><span class="rvts9">‚Äúexcess EEG correlation‚Äù</span><span class="rvts6"> across the Atlantic Ocean occurred only under the rotating </span><span class="rvts9">circumcerebral magnetic fields</span><span class="rvts6"> and not in control conditions</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=That%20human%20brains%20can%20display,within%20both%20cerebrums%20of%20a">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</a><span class="rvts6">. These human experiments bolstered the notion that a properly structured magnetic field can connect biological processes at a distance, potentially via a </span><span class="rvts11">‚Äúquantum neuroscience‚Äù</span><span class="rvts6"> mechanism</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=The%20aim%20of%20the%20present,generated%20as%20accelerating%20group%20velocities">scirp.org</a><a class="rvts10" href="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=According%20to%20Aczel%20,mediated%20and%20consequently%20diminishes%20in">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</a><span class="rvts6">.</span></p>
<p class=rvps2><span class="rvts6">Notably, these phenomena are subtle and required careful statistical analysis to verify. Yet the convergence of evidence ‚Äì photon emission bursts, pH shifts, EEG coherence ‚Äì all tied to </span><span class="rvts9">synchronized EM field exposures</span><span class="rvts6"> ‚Äì points to a real, underlying effect. The common thread is the use of </span><span class="rvts9">low-intensity (microTesla-range), frequency-changing magnetic fields</span><span class="rvts6"> as a kind of ‚Äúinvisible cable‚Äù linking separate systems. In all cases, standard electromagnetic theory alone would not predict any influence at such distances (especially with static or slowly changing fields that do not radiate power significantly). Thus, the authors invoke concepts like </span><span class="rvts9">non-locality</span><span class="rvts6"> and </span><span class="rvts9">entanglement</span><span class="rvts6"> analogies. As one introduction explains, an </span><span class="rvts9">‚Äúexcess correlation‚Äù</span><span class="rvts6"> means the correlation between two separated systems exceeds what any classical local mechanism could account for</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=some%20systematic%20manner%20as%20a,the%20parity%20of%20one%20photon">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</a><span class="rvts6">. If no normal signal or force is mediating the interaction (which drops off with distance), the implication is a form of entanglement ‚Äì i.e. a shared state that transcends spatial separation</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=According%20to%20Aczel%20,mediated%20and%20consequently%20diminishes%20in">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</a><a class="rvts10" href="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=some%20systematic%20manner%20as%20a,the%20parity%20of%20one%20photon">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</a><span class="rvts6">.</span></p>
<p class=rvps4><span class="rvts8">Angular Magnetic Field Configurations and ‚ÄúMacro-Entanglement‚Äù</span></p>
<p class=rvps2><span class="rvts6">How do these EM field setups work, and what is special about them? A point of convergence in these papers is the </span><span class="rvts9">precise configuration</span><span class="rvts6"> of the magnetic fields used to induce the correlations. They are far from simple static fields; rather, they involve </span><span class="rvts9">complex temporal patterns</span><span class="rvts6"> ‚Äì typically a </span><span class="rvts9">rotating or oscillating magnetic field whose frequency is not constant but accelerates or decelerates over time</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=peroxide%20and%20hypochlorite,exposed%20to%20the%20same%20precise">scirp.org</a><span class="rvts6">. In the 2012 photon experiment, for instance, the field was described as </span><span class="rvts11">‚Äúaccelerating group velocities containing decreasing phase modulations, followed by decelerating group velocities with increasing phase modulations‚Äù</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=peroxide%20and%20hypochlorite,exposed%20to%20the%20same%20precise">scirp.org</a><span class="rvts6">. In plainer terms, the magnetic field was made to rotate (circle) around an axis, with the rotation speed ramping up then down according to a programmed sequence. This created a very specific </span><span class="rvts9">time-space pattern</span><span class="rvts6"> that both locations experienced. Persinger‚Äôs team often refers to this as a </span><span class="rvts9">‚Äúcircumcerebral‚Äù or toroidal field with changing angular velocity‚Äù</span><span class="rvts6">, and they found it critical that the two separated targets </span><span class="rvts9">share the </span><span class="rvts12">same</span><span class="rvts9"> time-varying pattern</span><span class="rvts6"> (though not necessarily in phase) to exhibit the excess correlation</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=found%20that%20when%20two%20chemiluminescent,showed%20that%20two%20beakers%20of">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</a><a class="rvts10" href="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=Entanglement%20requires%20conditions%20in%20which,Consequently,%20even%20at%20great">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</a><span class="rvts6">. The hypothesis is that by imposing the same </span><span class="rvts11">informational structure</span><span class="rvts6"> on the EM field in two places, those places in effect share a common reference frame or </span><span class="rvts9">overlapping ‚Äúphase space‚Äù</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=In%202002%20Persinger,%20Roll,%20Tiller,,than%2020%20nT%20over%20most">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</a><a class="rvts10" href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=with%20a%20marked%20decrease%20in,weak,%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</a><span class="rvts6">. Persinger et al. have theorized that if two particles or brains share some </span><span class="rvts11">properties in space-time</span><span class="rvts6"> (through a coordinated field exposure), they can become a single functional system such that </span><span class="rvts9">activation of one immediately affects the other</span><span class="rvts6"> regardless of distance</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=Entanglement%20requires%20conditions%20in%20which,Consequently,%20even%20at%20great">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</a><span class="rvts6">.</span></p>
<p class=rvps2><span class="rvts6">Mathematically, this idea is inspired by the quantum entanglement analogy: in quantum experiments, two entities can behave as one system if they originated from a common source or interaction (e.g. a pair of photons with entangled polarization). Here, instead of a common origin, the </span><span class="rvts9">common EM field</span><span class="rvts6"> plays the role of the entangling interaction. It ‚Äúimprints‚Äù a shared state onto two separate targets. Indeed, the Dotta &amp; Persinger (2012) paper explicitly frames its goal as discerning if an </span><span class="rvts11">‚Äúentanglement-like‚Äù</span><span class="rvts6"> effect could be achieved for macroscopic systems by using magnetic fields</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=The%20aim%20of%20the%20present,generated%20as%20accelerating%20group%20velocities">scirp.org</a><span class="rvts6">. The positive results led them to conclude that </span><span class="rvts11">‚Äúspecific angular velocities‚Äù</span><span class="rvts6"> of magnetic fields can </span><span class="rvts9">transiently superimpose two non-local spaces into the same locus</span><span class="rvts6"> (figuratively speaking), doubling the reaction intensity as if both samples were together</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=found%20that%20when%20two%20chemiluminescent,showed%20that%20two%20beakers%20of">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</a><span class="rvts6">. In later work, Rouleau &amp; Persinger (2015) even detected a slight </span><span class="rvts9">disturbance in the local magnetic environment</span><span class="rvts6"> during these experiments: a tiny </span><span class="rvts9">1‚Äì5 nT diminishment in the ambient field intensity (along the East‚ÄìWest axis) was consistently observed when the excess correlation field sequence was active</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=54135#:~:text=,excess%20correlation%20sequence%20which">scirp.org</a><span class="rvts6">. This small anomaly, oriented in a specific direction, hinted that the process might involve subtle energy redistribution possibly via the geomagnetic field or an Aharonov-Bohm-type potential effect. They provided quantitative solutions suggesting that the energy associated with those 1‚Äì5 nT field changes could account for the information transfer, proposing a potential mechanism for the coupling</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=54135#:~:text=,excess%20correlation%20sequence%20which">scirp.org</a><span class="rvts6">.</span></p>
<p class=rvps2><span class="rvts6">Another important aspect is </span><span class="rvts9">‚Äúpoint durations‚Äù</span><span class="rvts6"> or timing: the 2014 Rouleau, Carniello &amp; Persinger study reported that the non-local pH shifts occurred in discrete steps at certain time points, implying a kind of quantized interaction or resonance window</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=site%20was%20proportional%20to%20the,no%20evidence%20of%20excess%20correlation">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</a><span class="rvts6">. The field pattern was composed of specific frequency segments, and the effects seemed to manifest when certain thresholds were met (e.g. field applied for X minutes). This resonates with the idea that the phenomenon might rely on achieving a particular coherence or phase alignment ‚Äì much like how quantum entanglement requires specific preparation. In practical terms, the researchers found they could ‚Äúprogram‚Äù the space with information: </span><span class="rvts9">storing a pattern and later retrieving it</span><span class="rvts6">. One paper described how </span><span class="rvts9">‚Äúincremental shifts in pH can be stored as ‚Äòspace-memory‚Äô and retrieved by re-applying the same rotating magnetic field‚Äù</span><span class="rvts6"> (Dotta et al., NeuroQuantology 2013) ‚Äì essentially suggesting the experimental chamber itself retained an imprint of the previous trial that could be reactivated</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=site%20was%20proportional%20to%20the,has%20recently%20been%20demonstrated%20by">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</a><span class="rvts6">. Such claims, while speculative, underscore a common theoretical framework: </span><span class="rvts9">space-time and EM fields can carry persistent information</span><span class="rvts6"> that links events across time and distance.</span></p>
<p class=rvps4><span class="rvts8">Theoretical Frameworks: From Quantum Potentials to ‚ÄúInformation Fields‚Äù</span></p>
<p class=rvps2><span class="rvts6">What physics might underpin these surprising results? The authors draw from a mix of quantum theory, classical EM field theory, and novel hypotheses. One recurring concept is the </span><span class="rvts9">Aharonov‚ÄìBohm effect</span><span class="rvts6">, which shows that electromagnetic </span><span class="rvts9">potentials</span><span class="rvts6"> (denoted </span><span class="rvts9">A</span><span class="rvts6"> for vector potential and </span><span class="rvts9">œÜ</span><span class="rvts6"> for scalar potential) can influence quantum systems even in regions where the classical electric and magnetic </span><span class="rvts9">fields</span><span class="rvts6"> are zero. This principle ‚Äì that potentials carry physical information not captured by E and B fields alone ‚Äì inspires attempts to use potentials for communication or entanglement. For example, Hal Puthoff‚Äôs 1998 </span><span class="rvts9">U.S. Patent 5,845,220</span><span class="rvts6"> proposes a method of transmitting information by creating fluctuations in the EM potentials without radiating conventional EM waves</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=apparatus%20relying%20on%20transmission%20of,of%20vector%20and%20scalar%20potentials">patents.google.com</a><span class="rvts6">. The patent describes a transmitter that drives a solenoid coil and parallel plates in tandem such that their emitted fields cancel out, producing a </span><span class="rvts9">‚Äúcurl-free vector potential‚Äù</span><span class="rvts6"> signal that can propagate to a receiver undisturbed</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=variations%20of%20signal%20source%2011,the%20signal%20of%20source%2011">patents.google.com</a><a class="rvts10" href="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=,from%20the%20solenoid%20and%20plates">patents.google.com</a><span class="rvts6">. </span><span class="rvts9">Figure:</span><span class="rvts6"> Below is a simplified depiction of the kind of coil apparatus used in such experiments ‚Äì essentially a toroidal (closed-loop) coil made by wrapping copper wire around a plastic ring </span><span class="rvts11">Researchers have used similar coils (often around a subject‚Äôs head or a beaker) to generate the specially patterned magnetic fields that produce non-local effects.</span><span class="rvts6"> By adjusting currents and voltages, Puthoff‚Äôs system suppresses the far-field radiation, creating a near-field zone where only the </span><span class="rvts9">potential</span><span class="rvts6"> oscillates</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=containing%20the%20information%20of%20signal,vector%20potential,%20as%20well%20as">patents.google.com</a><a class="rvts10" href="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=time%20and%20space.%20,structures%20which%20generate%20potential%20distributions">patents.google.com</a><span class="rvts6">. In theory, this </span><span class="rvts9">potential-only signal</span><span class="rvts6"> can penetrate through conductive media (like seawater or Faraday cages) that would normally block E and B fields</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=scalar%20potentials,%20with%20a%20suppressed,be%20established,%20as%20occurs%20for">patents.google.com</a><a class="rvts10" href="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=type.%20,so%20that%20the%20Josephson%20junction">patents.google.com</a><span class="rvts6">. The receiver in the patent is a </span><span class="rvts9">quantum interference device</span><span class="rvts6"> (a Josephson junction sensor) that can detect changes in the potential by the phase shifts they induce</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=scalar%20potentials%20(A,%20‚Åá%20),superconductors%20in%20an%20appropriate%20cryogenic">patents.google.com</a><span class="rvts6">. This approach is essentially trying to leverage the ‚Äúspooky‚Äù part of electromagnetism ‚Äì the potentials ‚Äì to send information in a way that is stealthy (no easily detectable fields) and possibly less distance-limited.</span></p>
<p class=rvps2><span class="rvts6">The Persinger-style experiments, while not explicitly using the language of Aharonov‚ÄìBohm, align with this philosophy. By using </span><span class="rvts9">quasi-static, low-frequency magnetic fields</span><span class="rvts6">, they minimize radiated energy (so classical signal strength doesn‚Äôt limit range) and possibly engage the potential domain. In fact, the devices and coil setups in these studies are remarkably simple: often just a pair of computer-controlled electromagnetic coils and/or electrodes creating a </span><span class="rvts9">toroidal magnetic field</span><span class="rvts6"> around the test subjects. The emphasis is on the timing and pattern of the field rather than field strength (usually only on the order of </span><span class="rvts9">10^-7 to 10^-6 Tesla</span><span class="rvts6">, comparable to Earth‚Äôs geomagnetic field). These weak, complex fields have been described as having </span><span class="rvts11">‚Äúastronomical degrees of freedom‚Äù</span><span class="rvts6"> in terms of information encoding</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://www.researchgate.net/publication/10967564_Possible_Disruption_of_Remote_Viewing_by_Complex_Weak_Magnetic_Fields_around_the_Stimulus_Site_and_the_Possibility_of_Accessing_Real_Phase_Space_A_Pilot_Study#:~:text=,by%20weak,%20infinitely%20variable">researchgate.net</a><a class="rvts10" href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=the%20present%20experiment%20the%20photographs,weak,%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</a><span class="rvts6">. Koren &amp; Persinger (2002) noted that an extremely </span><span class="rvts9">complex, weak magnetic noise field</span><span class="rvts6"> can act as a masker or disruptor of subtle information processes</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=the%20present%20experiment%20the%20photographs,weak,%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</a><a class="rvts10" href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=with%20a%20marked%20decrease%20in,weak,%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</a><span class="rvts6">. In their remote-viewing pilot study, they found that when a target object was immersed in a computer-generated 20 nT magnetic field that had </span><span class="rvts11">virtually infinite variability</span><span class="rvts6"> (Windows OS introduced random phase jitter), the renowned psychic Ingo Swann‚Äôs accuracy in describing the target dropped markedly</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=time%20of%20objects%20or%20events,modulated%20the%20actual">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</a><a class="rvts10" href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=wave%20form%20resulting%20in%20an,weak,%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</a><span class="rvts6">. By contrast, a simpler but otherwise identical field (generated by DOS, which did not add extra phase noise) did not impair his performance</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=the%20present%20experiment%20the%20photographs,weak,%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</a><span class="rvts6">. The implication is that the </span><span class="rvts9">information accessed during ‚Äúparanormal‚Äù perception was being carried by some real physical signal</span><span class="rvts6"> ‚Äì one that could be scrambled by magnetic noise</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=the%20present%20experiment%20the%20photographs,weak,%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</a><span class="rvts6">. They suggest that </span><span class="rvts9">information might reside in the structure of space-time around objects</span><span class="rvts6"> (a concept akin to an information field or Akashic record in mystic terms, but approached physically). Ingo Swann‚Äôs brain, in their model, was directly coupling to the target‚Äôs information field (bypassing normal senses), and a complex magnetic field effectively jammed that coupling</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=time%20of%20objects%20or%20events,Whereas%20the%20DOS%20software">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</a><a class="rvts10" href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12509207/#:~:text=wave%20form%20resulting%20in%20an,weak,%20infinitely%20variable%20magnetic%20fields">pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</a><span class="rvts6">. This idea meshes with Persinger‚Äôs broader </span><span class="rvts9">‚Äúquantum neuroscience‚Äù</span><span class="rvts6"> hypothesis: that brain activity can become quantum-coherent or entangled with external systems under certain conditions</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=The%20aim%20of%20the%20present,generated%20as%20accelerating%20group%20velocities">scirp.org</a><span class="rvts6">.</span></p>
<p class=rvps2><span class="rvts6">Another theoretical framework in these papers is the concept of </span><span class="rvts9">‚Äúvirtual photon‚Äù or zero-point field coupling</span><span class="rvts6">. Persinger and others have speculated that if two separated brains or cells are sharing an electromagnetic environment, they might be exchanging signals via the electromagnetic vacuum or bursts of photons. In fact, Dotta et al. (2012) measured actual </span><span class="rvts9">photon emissions from cells and brains</span><span class="rvts6"> and found hints that two separate entities could synchronize their ultra-weak light emissions when entangled by a magnetic field</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=The%20aim%20of%20the%20present,generated%20as%20accelerating%20group%20velocities">scirp.org</a><a class="rvts10" href="https://www.scirp.org/journal/paperinformation?paperid=17181#:~:text=Evidence%20of%20macroscopic%20quantum%20entanglement,Physical%20Review%20Letters,%2087">scirp.org</a><span class="rvts6">. This raises the possibility that the magnetic field may induce a kind of </span><span class="rvts9">coherent state</span><span class="rvts6"> where the systems exchange photons or energy in a phase-locked manner (sometimes called ‚Äúbiophoton‚Äù communication</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=within%20the%20pair,that%20the%20reaction%20was%20twice">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</a><span class="rvts6">). While still speculative, the convergence of these ideas is that </span><span class="rvts11">information transfer does not necessarily require classical energy transfer</span><span class="rvts6">. Instead, </span><span class="rvts9">coherence and shared reference frames</span><span class="rvts6"> allow distributed systems to behave as one.</span></p>
<p class=rvps2><span class="rvts6">In summary, the common theoretical outlook is that there exist </span><span class="rvts9">unconventional channels of communication</span><span class="rvts6"> in physical systems ‚Äì mediated by </span><span class="rvts11">fields, potentials, or quantum correlations</span><span class="rvts6"> ‚Äì which can be harnessed with the right engineering. Whether one frames it as ‚Äúmacroscopic entanglement‚Äù</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=The%20general%20consensus%20is%20that,rotating">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</a><span class="rvts6">, ‚Äúexcess correlation‚Äù</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/2142/f425e03c04d9254e208b065326ee571533b4.pdf#:~:text=some%20systematic%20manner%20as%20a,the%20parity%20of%20one%20photon">pdfs.semanticscholar.org</a><span class="rvts6">, ‚Äúscalar wave signaling‚Äù</span><a class="rvts10" href="https://patents.google.com/patent/US5845220A/en#:~:text=apparatus%20relying%20on%20transmission%20of,of%20vector%20and%20scalar%20potentials">patents.google.com</a><span class="rvts6">, or ‚Äúspace-memory encoding‚Äù, all these works converge on the notion that </span><span class="rvts9">information can be embedded in the structure of a field and retrieved elsewhere without a classical signal</span><span class="rvts6">. This blurs the line between physics and information: the field configuration </span><span class="rvts11">is</span><span class="rvts6"> the message. And if the receiver is tuned to the same configuration, it will respond accordingly.</span></p>
